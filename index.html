Top 5 Comebacks of Soccer History

This page presents the most iconic comebacks in soccer history.

They include the following:

  1. Champions League Comebacks
    • Barcelona v. PSG
    • Liverpool v. AC Milan
    • Manchester United v. Bayern
  2. World Cup Comebacks
    • Portugal v. North Korea
  3. World Cup Qualifiers Comebacks
    • Germany v. Sweden

1. Barcelona against PSG (2017)

In the Champions League Round of 16, Barcelona overturned a shocking 4–0 first-leg loss with an incredible 6–1 win at the Camp Nou. The final goal came in stoppage time, completing a 6–5 aggregate win in what many call the greatest comeback ever.

2. Liverpool against AC Milan (2005)

In the Champions League Final in Istanbul, Liverpool were down 3–0 at halftime. In just six second-half minutes, they made it 3–3, then won in a dramatic penalty shootout. It's one of the most iconic matches in soccer history.

3. Germany against Sweden (2012)

In a World Cup qualifier, Germany led 4–0 with just 30 minutes left. Sweden mounted an outrageous comeback to draw 4–4 in Berlin — one of the biggest collapses in international qualifying history.

4. Portugal against North Korea (1966)

In the World Cup quarterfinal, North Korea took a 3–0 lead by the 25th minute, shocking Portugal. Then Eusébio went off, scoring four of Portugal's five unanswered goals to win 5–3. One of the earliest and wildest World Cup turnarounds.

5. Manchester United against Bayern Munich (1999)

In the Champions League Final, United were down 1–0 for 90 minutes. In stoppage time, they scored twice to win 2–1 and lift the trophy. Not a comeback across two legs, but the timing and stakes make it unforgettable.
